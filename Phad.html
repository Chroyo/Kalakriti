Phad Art

Phad painting is a traditional form of scroll painting from Rajasthan, particularly associated with the Bhilwara region. These vibrant artworks are used as visual storytelling tools and depict the heroic deeds of folk deities like Pabuji and Devnarayan. Painted on long pieces of cloth or canvas, Phad paintings are usually carried by Bhopas (priest-singers) who narrate the story using these visuals. They are characterized by bold lines, earthy colors, and a strong sense of narrative. The art is a blend of religion, folklore, and oral traditions.
