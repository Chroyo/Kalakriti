Warli Art

Warli art is a tribal art form from Maharashtra, traditionally created by the Warli tribe. Known for its iconic white-on-mud style, it uses simple geometric shapes like circles, triangles, and lines to depict scenes of everyday life, nature, and rituals. Warli paintings are deeply rooted in tribal customs, often showcasing farming, dancing, hunting, and spiritual themes. Traditionally, these were drawn on hut walls using rice paste, but the style has now gained global popularity.
