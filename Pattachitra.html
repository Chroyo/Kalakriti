Pattachitra Art

Pattachitra is a traditional cloth-based scroll painting that originated in Odisha and West Bengal. The word "Pattachitra" comes from the Sanskrit words "patta" (cloth) and "chitra" (picture). These paintings are known for their intricate details, mythological narratives, and rich natural colors. Artists use fine brushes and natural pigments to depict stories from epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata, often bordered with ornamental floral designs. The art is deeply spiritual and is also used to depict the rituals of the Jagannath Temple in Puri.
