Madhubani Art

Madhubani, also known as Mithila painting, is a traditional folk art form from the Mithila region of Bihar, India. It is characterized by intricate patterns, bold colors, and themes of nature, mythology, and daily life. Artists often use natural dyes and pigments, and paintings are done with fingers, twigs, brushes, or even matchsticks. This art is deeply rooted in cultural rituals and festivals, especially weddings and religious ceremonies.
